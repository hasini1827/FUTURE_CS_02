# Phishing Email Detection & Awareness System

## Overview
This repository contains my submission for **Cyber Security Task 2** by Future Interns. The project focuses on acting as a security analyst to analyze phishing email samples, identify malicious indicators, and develop a comprehensive awareness report to educate non-technical users on avoiding cyber attacks.

## Objective
- Analyze real-world phishing email samples.
- Identify common phishing indicators and red flags.
- Classify email risks (Safe / Suspicious / Phishing).
- Explain attack mechanisms in simple, non-technical language.
- Provide actionable prevention and awareness guidelines for end-users.

## Tools & Resources Used
- **Email Header Analysis**: [Google Admin Toolbox Messageheader](https://toolbox.googleapps.com/apps/messageheader/), [MXToolbox](https://mxtoolbox.com/EmailHeaders.aspx)
- **Browser Tools**: Safe inspection of links, domains, and URLs.
- **Documentation**: MS Word / PDF
- **Data Sources**: Publicly available phishing email repositories (used strictly for study and analysis).

## Analysis Approach
My methodology for analyzing the phishing emails followed a structured, step-by-step approach:
1. **Sample Collection**: Sourced realistic phishing email examples from trusted educational datasets.
2. **Header Analysis**: Inspected email headers to uncover the true sender domain, spoofed addresses, and routing paths.
3. **Domain & Link Inspection**: Carefully examined the body text, identifying fake or suspicious sender domains and inspecting embedded URLs safely.
4. **Indicator Identification**: Flagged key red flags such as urgency/fear-based language, generic greetings, and suspicious links.
5. **Risk Classification**: Categorized the analyzed emails clearly to define their threat level.
6. **Documentation & Reporting**: Compiled findings into a structured format.
7. **Awareness Creation**: Developed Do's and Don'ts and prevention guidelines aimed at employees and regular users.
